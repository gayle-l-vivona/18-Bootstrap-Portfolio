US Forest Service

Group project. Redesign the US Forest Service webpage and mobile app.

  • Problem: Too much unnecessary and outdated information, no focus, and poor navigation.
  • The redesign focused on the primary users: the public interested in outdoor activities.
  • New design with a robust search engine makes it easy to find a national forest with the desired activities and facilities.
  • My role: UX-UI co-designer: wireframing, user testing.
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Miro, Trello, Google Suite, InVision

EYE-tinerary

Individual project. San Francisco mobile travel app for the blind and visually impaired.

  • Problem: Information on accessibility is not always readily available, or is not accurate.
  • EYE-tinerary is designed for use with the accessibility features built into the iPhone.
  • An interactive accessibility rating system differentiates EYE-tinerary from the competition.
  • My role: UX designer: wireframing, user testing, A/B testing, guerilla testing, prototyping.
  • Tools: Adobe XD, InVision, Miro, Whimsical

Bike East Bay

Group project. Redesign the Bike East Bay non-profit webpage and mobile app.

  • Problem: Outdated format and poor navigation. Simple tasks are difficult to complete.
  • The redesign focused on 4 tasks: making a donation, becoming a member, signing up for classes, and signing up to volunteer.
  • New navigation, a de-cluttered layout and redesigned forms make it easy to find information and complete tasks.
  • My role: UX-UI co-designer: wireframing, user testing. Primary designer: Volunteer process.
  • Tools: Adobe XD, InVision, Whimsical, Google Forms, Miro, Trello
